Keynote Speaker

Dr. Nicole A. Cooke

We are excited to announce Dr. Nicole A. Cooke, the Augusta Baker Endowed Chair and an associate professor at the School of Library and Information Science, as our keynote speaker.

Dr. Cooke’s research and teaching interests include human information behavior, fake news consumption and resistance, critical cultural information studies, and diversity and social justice in librarianship.

Dr. Cooke's talk will include confessions from a former Doctoral Student, now a Doctoral Educator. Dr. Cooke will share her thoughts on the LIS pipeline, self care, and her work with the Spectrum Doctoral Fellowship Program.

For more information about Dr. Nicole A. Cooke, please check out her profile page on the University of South Carolina website.
